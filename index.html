Andrew Robert Likoudis

Andrew Likoudis is a strategy consultant, editor, and researcher whose work spans nonprofit development, institutional strategy, editorial management, and research communications. He holds an M.A. from Franciscan University of Steubenville and a B.S. in Communication Studies from Towson University.

As founder and chairman of the Likoudis Legacy Foundation—a 501(c)(3) research institute—he has built organizational infrastructure from the ground up: establishing governance, recruiting advisory councils, launching a peer-reviewed journal, and managing multi-contributor scholarly projects. His professional background includes fellowships with McKinsey & Co., Goldman Sachs 10,000 Small Businesses, and Johns Hopkins University.

He provides consulting, research, organizational strategy, and communications services to nonprofits, foundations, publishers, and institutions across sectors.

Strategy & Consulting

Andrew Likoudis is a strategy consultant, editor, and researcher whose work spans nonprofit development, institutional strategy, editorial management, and research communications. He brings cross-sector experience in organizational governance, multi-stakeholder project management, and policy analysis.

His major editorial project is a 40-chapter scholarly anthology he organized and edited independently—coordinating 30+ contributors across three continents, managing a multi-year publication pipeline, negotiating publisher contracts, and overseeing the endorsement and approval process from acquisition through publication. He has edited or compiled 10 book-length volumes in total.

As founder and chairman of the Likoudis Legacy Foundation, he built a 501(c)(3) research institute from the ground up—establishing governance structures, recruiting a nine-person advisory council, launching a fellows program, and founding The Kydones Review, a peer-reviewed journal of which he serves as editor-in-chief.

He is co-founding the Orientale Lumen Institute to offer an accredited online graduate degree, as well as certificate programs for professionals engaged in cross-cultural and interreligious dialogue.

His institutional experience includes three years on the Archdiocesan Pastoral Council of Baltimore—the young adult representative on a 14-member council—one of four at-large, archdiocese-wide appointments—advising on institutional restructuring, strategic planning, and youth engagement, supporting a $5 million capital campaign at the Cathedral of Mary Our Queen, professional development with McKinsey and Co.'s Forward program (Oct.–Dec. 2025), a Goldman Sachs 10,000 Small Businesses fellowship developing marketing strategy and client acquisition for a government contracting firm, and a Johns Hopkins University fellowship producing policy memos and procurement research for a Bloomberg Philanthropies–backed workforce development initiative, in collaboration with Julep Consulting.

His communications work includes 40+ published articles across national outlets including the National Catholic Register, Philosophy Now, and EWTN News, covering policy, governance, technology ethics, and institutional affairs. He has conducted exclusive interviews with senior institutional leaders and provided field coverage of national conferences.

Andrew is an active member of his local community in Baltimore, where he has been engaged in civic and institutional life for several years.

Beyond consulting, Andrew draws on nearly a decade of hospitality and operations experience. He has welcomed hundreds of guests as an Airbnb Superhost and has served at Charleston, one of OpenTable's Top 10 restaurants in America. He enjoys kayaking, bachata, and chess.
